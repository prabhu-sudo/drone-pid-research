# Week 4 — Final Assembly & First Hover

## Summary

Completed final assembly: mounted the receiver, mounted and secured the battery, routed and zip-tied all wiring, and bolted the FC+ESC stack to lock it in place on the frame. Finished final Betaflight setup, confirmed arm mode worked correctly, and performed the first controlled hover test.

## Work Done

- Mounted the FlySky FS-iA10B receiver to the frame.
- Mounted the battery using foam tape padding, secured with a battery strap.
- Routed all wiring (motor leads, receiver signal wire, battery leads) and secured with zip ties for cable management and to keep wires clear of props/airflow.
- Bolted the FC+ESC stack down through the grommets to lock the stack in place on the frame standoffs.
- Completed final Betaflight configuration pass.
- No FPV camera/VTX fitted — this build is flown line-of-sight (LOS), not goggle FPV.

## First Power-On & Hover Test

- Tested arm mode — armed correctly on the first attempt, no issues.
- Performed the first hover test indoors, in a controlled setting, at low throttle only. Lifted off briefly and held a stable low hover before landing.

## Notes

Arming working cleanly on the first try, with no further debugging needed, was a good sign that the Week 3 fixes (wiring, firmware, failsafe) were genuinely resolved and not just partially patched. The conservative, low-throttle, indoor approach to the first hover was a deliberate safety choice — confirm basic flight behavior in a controlled space before attempting outdoor or higher-throttle flight.
